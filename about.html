About Us





Quick Overview

The Karakulam Co-operative Society was registered on 03/06/1950 and its functioning was started on 01/08/1950. The founder of this organization was Mr. Velappan Nair and also he was the first president of this organization. The Karakulam Co-operative Society has its working jurisdiction that covers the whole area of Karakulam Grama Panchayath. The society has its headquarters at Karakulam. The main objective of the Society is to encourage tactical and cooperative behaviour among the members and also the society promote cooperative principles and develops plans to bring it into the best possible way. For providing agricultural, industrial, employment, educational, and trade loans to our members, the society borrows loans from Government, NABARD, NCDC, State Co-operative Bank, District Co-operative Bank, and other financial institutions. More than that, society is also organizing activities for improving members’ finances and family life. The society is taking special care to organize activities that promote the cottage industry and small industries

What is Co-operative ?

A cooperative, or co-op, is an organization or business entity owned and operated by a group of individuals who have a shared interest or goal. Unlike traditional businesses where profits are distributed to shareholders, cooperatives are typically focused on meeting the needs and interests of their members.

What is Co-operative Bank ?

A cooperative bank is a financial institution owned and controlled by its members, who are also its customers. Unlike traditional banks, which are driven by profit and shareholder interests, cooperative banks operate on the principle of democratic member control, where each member typically has one vote regardless of their investment level. They focus on serving the needs of their members, often offering favorable rates and lower fees, and reinvesting profits into the bank or distributing them among members.

Difference of cooperative bank ?

Unlike traditional banks that are driven by profit and shareholder interests, cooperative banks prioritize the needs of their members. They offer democratic control with one member, one vote, and often reinvest profits into the bank or distribute them among members, rather than focusing solely on maximizing shareholder returns.
